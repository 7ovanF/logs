A word is the processor's natural data unit.\
Word = the size of general-purpose registers and the width of ALU datapath.\
However, MIPS uses byte addresses, so sequential words differ by 4. (why?)\
Part of the reason: C compatibility.\
1 word: 4 bytes/32bits.

---

each array element occupies a word.

example:

C Code: `A[7] = x + A[10];`

MIPS code:
``` MIPS
lw $t0, 40($s3)
add $t0, $s2, $s3
sw $t0, 28($s3)
```
note here, word cannot be not a multiple of 4 (that would mean taking the middle of the word)

> there is also ulw (unaligned load word; for adresses unaligned with 4)
> (are pseudoinstructions)

Store word:
`sw rs rd`
